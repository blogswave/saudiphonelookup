Understanding Spam and Scam Calls

Spam and scam calls are different, although they may appear similar at first.

Spam Calls: These are often from telemarketers promoting products or services. While not necessarily dangerous, they can be disruptive and annoying.

Scam Calls: These are fraudulent calls aimed at deceiving the recipient into sharing sensitive information or sending money. Scammers may pose as banks, government agencies, or delivery services to trick people.

The first step in protecting yourself is understanding the different types of calls and the risks associated with each.

Common Types of Scam Calls in Saudi Arabia

Scammers use various tactics to target individuals. Some of the most common scam calls in Saudi Arabia include:

  • Banking and Financial Scams: Scammers pretend to be from a bank, claiming there is an issue with your account or a pending transaction. They ask for account details, passwords, or verification codes.
  • Lottery or Prize Scams: Calls claiming you’ve won a prize or lottery are common. Scammers ask for personal details or upfront payments to “claim” your prize.
  • Government Impersonation: Fraudsters sometimes pose as government officials, threatening fines or legal actions to pressure victims into giving sensitive information.
  • Delivery or E-commerce Scams: With the rise of online shopping, scammers may call claiming there’s an issue with a delivery and request sensitive information.

Signs That a Call May Be a Scam

  • Pressure to act immediately
  • Requests for personal or financial information
  • Threats of legal action or fines
  • Promises of money or prizes that seem too good to be true
  • Numbers that appear unfamiliar or international

Always take a moment to analyze the call. If it raises suspicion, it’s safer to verify the number first.

How to Protect Yourself from Scam Calls

  1. Do Not Share Personal Information: Never provide sensitive data, such as passwords, verification codes, or bank details, over the phone unless you are certain of the caller’s identity.
  2. Use Call-Blocking Features: Most smartphones have built-in options to block unwanted numbers. You can also install apps specifically designed to identify and block spam calls.
  3. Verify Unknown Numbers: Before calling back or answering, use online lookup tools like number book to check the identity and reputation of unknown numbers.
  4. Stay Calm and Avoid Urgency: Scammers often create a sense of urgency to pressure you into making quick decisions. Take your time to verify before responding.
  5. Report Suspicious Calls: Many telecommunication authorities in Saudi Arabia provide channels for reporting fraudulent calls. Reporting helps protect yourself and others in the community.
